Design and Manufacturing of a Cooling Tower

(November 2022   ~   March 2023)

This project showcases a miniature version of an industrial cooling tower, designed and manufactured by our project group as part of the ME 310: Heat Transfer Equipment Design Sessional course. The primary objective of this work was to simulate and demonstrate the heat exchange process between incoming air and process water, effectively highlighting the principles of thermal energy transfer in industrial applications.
Our project involved a comprehensive approach, starting from the conceptual design and material selection to the assembly and testing phases. It served as a practical implementation of theoretical knowledge gained during the course, providing valuable insights into the dynamics of heat transfer. The key features of this project include:

  • SS body parts, SS box pipes for strengthening the core, and exhaust fan at top for creating forced-drought configuration.

  • 2 types of packing materials (Splash and Film fills) for enlarging the area of heat transfer.

  • Assessment of temperature drop using a K-type thermocouple.

Bio-Inspired Robot: RC ROBOFISH

(May 2022   ~   September 2022)

This project was undertaken as part of my ME 366: Electro-mechanical System Design Sessional course, with the objective of modeling and manufacturing a Nature-Inspired Robot. Collaborating with my project group, we developed an Arduino-based, remote-controlled robotic fish that mimics the movement patterns of that aquatic creature.
The design process involved extensive brainstorming, prototyping, programming, and testing to achieve a seamless blend of mechanical and electronic systems. The robot's functionality reflects a practical application of electromechanical principles, showcasing innovation in biomimicry and robotic control. The key aspects of this project were:

  • Replicating the caudal and pectoral movements of a real fish with the help of servo motors and other hardware components.

  • Developing an Arduino code to translate this movements to different parts and incorporating Bluetooth controlling of these movements using a smartphone.

  • Inserting a mechanical sliding mass mechanism for vertical gliding of the fishbot using proper torque and making the body auto-buoyant.
