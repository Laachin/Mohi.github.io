Intern

Electricity Generation Company of Bangladesh (EGCB)

(November 1, 2023   ~   November, 21 2023)

I completed a month-long industrial internship at the Electricity Generation Company of Bangladesh (EGCB), located in Siddhirganj, Narayanganj, Bangladesh. EGCB is one of the nationâ€™s leading contributors to the power grid system, operating a robust infrastructure that includes three distinct power plants: a 412 MW Combined Cycle Power Plant, a 335 MW Combined Cycle Power Plant, and two 120 MW Gas Turbine Power Plant (Peaking PP). During my internship, I undertook various responsibilities that provided comprehensive insights into power generation systems:

  • Process Analysis: I visited different components, systems, and devices involved in the power generation process, documenting detailed notes and creating flowcharts to map the entire operational workflow.

  • Team Collaboration: I worked in collaborative teams, reported daily updates to supervising officials, and participated in regular quizzes to assess and enhance my understanding of the systems.

  • Feasibility and Safety Assessment: I analyzed the feasibility of power generation processes at the plant, identified potential inefficiencies or drawbacks in specific systems, and reviewed past incidents to evaluate the adequacy of safety measures and accident management protocols.

This experience allowed me to gain practical knowledge about large-scale power generation, enhance my analytical and teamwork skills, and develop a deeper appreciation for the complexities of energy systems and their role in national infrastructure.

Industrial Field Visit

Haripur (HPL) 360 MW power station (China Southern Power Grid Co Ltd.)

(June 30, 2022)

As part of an initiative by the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), a day-long industrial field visit was organized for Mechanical Engineering students to the Haripur 360 MW Combined Cycle Powerplant, located in Bandar, Narayanganj, Bangladesh. The visit aimed to provide students with practical exposure to fundamental engineering principles related to power generation, mechanical processes, and the operation of heavy machinery.
During this visit, I had the opportunity to gain insights into various systems integral to power plant operations, including gas and steam turbines, heat recovery steam generators (HRSGs), generators, transformers, cooling water circulation systems, and water treatment plants, among others. This hands-on experience allowed me to connect theoretical concepts from my coursework to real-world applications, offering a unique perspective on the intricate workings of power generation infrastructure.
As an undergraduate student, this visit was an invaluable learning experience, enabling me to witness firsthand the complexities of large-scale mechanical systems and their role in energy production. It not only deepened my understanding of engineering principles but also reinforced my enthusiasm for exploring the practical aspects of my field.
