Research Experience

My research experiences can be broadly categorized into the following sections.

  • Undergraduate Thesis (June 2023  ~  June 2024):

    • My undergraduate thesis marked a pivotal point in my research journey, focusing on the computational assessment of a Scramjet (Supersonic Combustion Ramjet) Combustor. Under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Mohammad Ali, I used ANSYS Fluent to model and simulate a 2D, non-reactive flow field, utilizing the SST k-ω turbulence model and introducing an innovative quadruple cavity design to enhance overall performance and air-fuel mixing behavior.
    The research involved detailed quantitative analyses of key parameters such as mixing efficiency (ηm), total pressure recovery (Porec), and mass-averaged Mach number (Mavg), alongside qualitative studies of shock-induced flow characteristics and study of recirculations within the scramjet combustor.
    My analysis of the Scramjet Combustor can be serialized into three different sub-categories.

    1. Effects of using "Quadruple Cavities" on the Mixing Performance of a Hypersonic Scramjet Combustor

    Details

    In this part, I examined the influences of trapezoidal quadruple cavities, comparing with a previously studied dual-cavity configuration in terms of hypersonic performance parameters inside the scramjet engine. The idea was to visualize how an added pair of cavities affect the flow field, and assess the possible pros and cons of this setup. Accordingly, numerical solutions were generated for quadruple cavities of aspect ratio 7, and for different fuel-to-airstream pressure ratios (FAPR) (4.5, 9.0, 13.5, 18.0). The key observations of this work is summarized.

    • Quadruple cavity configuration provides a significantly higher air-fuel mixing at the combustor outlet compared to the dual cavity setup.

    • Overall mass-averaged mixing behavior is high for quadruple cavities at lower pressure ratios (FAPR 4.5, 9.0), but this parameter is more pronounced for dual cavities at higher pressure ratios (FAPR 13.5, 18.0).

    • The trade-off for thrust output at the combustor exit follows a similar pattern as dual cavities for the quadruple ones.

    • The interaction between opposing bow-shocks and the upstream separation shocks pose the most significant impact on the nature of upstream and downstream recirculation zones formed within the cavities, and accordingly, the mixing characteristics is varied .

    This study has been published in the International Journal of Engine Research (Q1, IF: 2.3, CS: 5.5). For a detailed overview of the work Visit Here.

    2. Effects of "Cavity Geometric Orientation" on the Mixing Performance Inside a Scramjet Combustor

    Details

    Here, I studied how the geometry and orientation of various cavities can affect the air-fuel mixing inside the scramjet combustor, and tried to figure out an orientation that provides better performance compared to others. Therefore, I examined four different quadruple cavity configurations namely, Trapezoidal-Trapezoidal (TT), Trapezoidal-Rectangular (TR), Rectangular-Trapezoidal (RT), and Rectangular-Rectangular (RR) configurations, where the first word denotes the geometry of upstream pair of cavities and the second word stands for the downstream pair. The numerical study obtained following conclusions.

    • The geometry of the upstream cavity has a more pronouned effect on the internal flow-field compared to the downstream cavities, as the separation shock ahead of upstream cavities and the opposing bow shocks originating from the injectors heavily impact along the combustor and affect the recirculations in this region.

    • RR and RT configurations show significantly superior performance compared to TT and TR configurations, with RR reaching the maximum of 74.5% mixing efficiency.

    • RT has a better pressure recovery in its downstream region compared to RR configuration owing to the adverse interactions between rebounded bow shocks and the shear layer in the downstream cavity region of RT.

    • Overall, the study shows that rectangular quadruple cavities might be a better choice compared to the trapezoidal quadruple cavities considering mixing performance. However, the design limitations and stress analysis of these designs are yet to be done regarding structural optimization.

    This work has been presented at the BSME International Conference on Thermal Engineering (BSME ICTE 2024, Dhaka), and is currently under process of being published.

    3. Effects of "Cavity Aspect Ratio" on the Mixing Performance of a Hypersonic Scramjet Combustor

    Details

    In this study, I examined rectangular quadruple cavities of different aspect ratios (Cavity Length to Depth ratio, L/D) to figure out their influence on the air fuel mixing inside the scramjet combustor. The idea was to visualize how the size and positioning of these cavities and fuel injectors affect the shock-induced flow field, and remark a critically better configuration among them. Accordingly, numerical solutions were generated for combustors with rectangular quadruple cavities of 3 different aspect ratios (AR): 3, 5, 7, and for different fuel-to-airstream pressure ratios (FAPR) (4.5, 9.0, 13.5, 18.0). The core understandings of this study are concluded as follows.

    • Rectangular Cavity-configuration with higher AR provides a significantly higher air-fuel mixing at the combustor outlet compared to lower AR configurations.

    • The shockwave characteristics of the internal flow-field follows a similar trend for most of the cases, and the shock-to-shear layer and shock-to-boundary layer interactions are attributed to similar outcomes at definite positions inside the scramjet combustor.

    • The more the AR, the more pronounced the effects of downstream cavity recirculation zones become. Hence, higher AR corresponds to better mixing, regardless of the FAPR.

    • There is a trade-off of thrust output and pressure recovery at the outlet of high AR configurations, as bigger recirculations and intrinsic shock train corresponds to adverse obstruction of natural flow field throughout the combustor. Therefore, lower AR offers better pressure recovery.

    • Rectangular quadruple cavities with AR 7 and FAPR 4.5 provided the best mixing behavior among the 12 cases studied.

  • Control in Thermal Management (November 2023  ~  March 2024):

    • Guided by Dr. Sumon Saha, I contributed to a research project applying control engineering principles to thermo-fluid systems, with a particular focus on the thermal management of forced convection in a ferrous micropolar fluid. Utilizing COMSOL Multiphysics, my team designed and modeled a 2D T-shaped domain of interest, implemented steady state fundamental equations of fluid dynamics, and invested temperature regulation through the incorporation of P, PI, and PID digital controllers.
    The study involved a comprehensive analysis of the controllers' performance, particularly their effects on overshoot and steady-state error, providing valuable insights into their applicability and effectiveness in thermo-fluid systems. This work enhanced my understanding of control strategies and highlighted their potential for optimizing thermal processes in engineering applications.

    Details

    The 2D design was modeled and our program was validated against pre-existed data. The controllers were then tuned for proper values of Proportional, Integral and Derivative gains (Kp , Ki , Kd). The non-dimensional temperature was plotted against non-dimensional time for tuning and accordingly, these values were recorded. Finally, temperature and velocity profiles were simulated for setups comprising a P controller; a P and I controller; and a P, I and D controller. It is evident that,

    • Incorporating a P controller provides fast reponse, but incurs some steady state error.

    • Implementing P and I controller eliminates steady-state error, but is responsible for higher overshoot.

    • Including P, I and D controllers together provides a fast response, zero steady state error, and improved stability/damping. However, tuning for PID controllers is the most time consuming and requires sensitive utilization.

    The findings of this research were compiled, presented at the International Conference on the Application of Fluid Dynamics (ICAFD 2024, Tamil Nadu), and is currently under process of publication.
