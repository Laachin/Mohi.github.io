Research Experience

My research experiences can be broadly categorized into the following sections.

  • Undergraduate Thesis (June 2023  ~  June 2024):

    • My undergraduate thesis marked a pivotal point in my research journey, focusing on the computational assessment of a hypersonic scramjet combustor. Under the supervision of Professor Mohammad Ali, I employed Ansys Fluent to model and simulate a 2D, non-reactive flow field, utilizing the SST k-ω turbulence model and introducing an innovative quadruple cavity design to enhance performance.
    The research involved detailed quantitative analyses of key parameters such as mixing efficiency and total pressure recovery, alongside qualitative studies of shock-induced flow characteristics within the combustor. These investigations not only advanced my understanding of hypersonic aerodynamics but also allowed me to develop proficiency in CFD tools and methodologies critical for tackling complex flow phenomena.
    The outcomes of this work led to three research papers: one accepted at the BSME ICTE 2024, and two currently under review in esteemed peer-reviewed journals. This experience solidified my passion for computational research and furthered my commitment to exploring cutting-edge challenges in CFD and relevant areas.

  • Implementation of Control Theory in Thermal Management (November 2023  ~  March 2024):

    • Guided by Dr. Sumon Saha, I contributed to a research project applying control engineering principles to thermo-fluid systems, with a particular focus on the thermal management of forced convection in a ferrous micropolar fluid. Utilizing COMSOL Multiphysics, my team designed and modeled a 2D T-shaped domain of interest, investigating temperature regulation through the implementation of P, PI, and PID digital controllers.
    The study involved a comprehensive analysis of the controllers' performance, particularly their effects on overshoot and steady-state error, providing valuable insights into their applicability and effectiveness in thermo-fluid systems. This work enhanced my understanding of control strategies and highlighted their potential for optimizing thermal processes in engineering applications.
    The findings of this research were compiled, submitted to the ICAFD 2024, and has been accepted for publication.
